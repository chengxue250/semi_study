Huawei unveils 122TB SSD that bypasses US 3D-NAND sanctions via die-on-board packaging 华为发布122TB SSD,以die-on-board封装绕开美国3D NAND管制

Tom's Hardware · 1d ago Tom's Hardware · 1天前

Huawei has introduced a 122TB enterprise SSD that avoids US-controlled high-layer-count 3D NAND by mounting the NAND dies directly on the PCB (die-on-board) rather than buying packaged chips. The trick gets more silicon area per drive while sidestepping the specific tooling and IP that are export-controlled. It does not solve the underlying density disadvantage, but it ships a real product that the sanctions architecture is supposed to prevent. 华为发布122TB企业级SSD,通过将NAND裸die直接贴装于PCB(die-on-board)的方式,避开美国管制的高层数3D NAND成品芯片。这一手法以更多每盘硅面积换取容量,同时绕开受管制的封装工艺与知识产权。它未解决底层的密度劣势,但实打实地推出了管制体系本应阻断的产品。
